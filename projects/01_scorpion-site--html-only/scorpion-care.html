Equipment

  • 10 gallon tank
  • Tank thermometer
  • Heating mat
  • Substrate / bedding. Some combination of the following
    • peat
    • soil
    • or even vermaculite
    • sphagum moss (not essential)
  • Variety of things to hide under
    • bark
    • smooth stones
    • broken plant pots
    • et cetera

Environment

How to set up the equipment

Tank

Heating mat

  • the recommended temperature is a gradient with one end of the tank 21° C, and the other 32° C
  • The gradient can be achieved by placing the heating mat so that it is only be under a third of the enclosure

    • Failure to have a renge of temperatures available could lead to your scorpion overheating

Substrate

Good substrates can be made using peat, soil or even vermiculite.

Whatever is chosen, it should be reach a depth of 6 - 12 cm.

Additionally an area of spagnum moss is recommended.

Diet

Food

They do well on a diet composed mainly of crickets, but it's good to throw in the odd mealworm, to give them a bit of variety.

They will also eat moths

Hydration

The best way to keep your scorpion hydrated is to keep the tank humid.

You can provide a shallow water dish, although it may become filled in during the scorpions activity.

Effect of UV light

When exposed to ultra violet many arthtopods glow.

This includes scorpions, of which the Emperor Scorpion is one of the best examples
